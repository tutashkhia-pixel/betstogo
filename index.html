Model & method.

Every number BETS→GO shows is computed from these rules — nothing is a black box. Core engines produce facts only; a single Decision Orchestrator turns facts into advice. Since v0.4 the fundamental unit of analysis is the portfolio: every bet is a payoff rule over the same joint score distribution, and every recommendation is justified by how it changes the whole portfolio.

0 · Portfolio engine (v0.4)

The in-play model produces a joint distribution over final scores. Each open bet maps every possible final score to a profit or loss. Summing those maps gives the portfolio's net P/L per outcome — from which come portfolio expected value, worst case, best case, exposure per result, and correlated-risk overlap. Verdicts (HOLD · ADD · REDUCE · HEDGE · REBALANCE · CASH OUT) are justified only by the portfolio delta. Path-dependent markets (next goal) and manual-probability bets contribute their expected value without a per-score map, and are labeled as such.

1 · In-play goal model

Remaining goals for each side follow a Poisson process. Base rate is the pre-match expected-goals pair (derived automatically from the Winner odds you enter) scaled by time remaining, with a late-game uplift (second halves produce ~55% of goals):

λ = xG × (90 − min)/90 × (1 + 0.20 × min/90)

  • Red cards: the short-handed side's rate ×0.72 per card; opponent ×1.18.
  • Momentum: each step shifts rates ±9% between the sides.
  • Game state: after 55', a trailing side pushes (+6% per goal down, max 2); the leader manages (−5%).

The joint distribution of additional goals (0–10 each side) prices every derivative market: 1X2, totals, team totals, handicaps, BTTS, next goal.

2 · Edge & EV

American odds convert to implied probability (+120 → 45.5%, −150 → 60%). Edge = model probability − implied probability. EV per $1 = p×(dec−1) − (1−p). Implied probabilities include the book's margin, so a small positive edge may just be vig — BETS→GO flags edges under 2 points as noise.

3 · Cash Out

Per bet: Expected Hold Value = P(bet wins) × total payout. Hard Rock's Cash Out offer is judged by ratio = offer / EHV: ≥1.05 take it · 0.97–1.05 taking part is fair · <0.88 the book is underpaying. Books price Cash Out below fair value on purpose; taking a sub-0.9 ratio repeatedly is a slow leak.

4 · Hedges & adds

A hedge is only recommended when it locks value against the portfolio's dominant exposure — never to soothe nerves. A new bet (ADD) is only recommended when its market clears a +4% EV bar on its own merit and improves the portfolio: higher expected value, smaller downside, or upside where the portfolio is currently negative. Suggested stake is quarter-Kelly, capped at 50% of committed capital. If nothing clears the bar, the correct trade is no trade.

5 · Decision Quality Score

0–100, scored at close: positive-edge entries (20) · committed ≤ 5% of bankroll (15) · followed the Cash Out engine (20) · no negative-EV adds (20) · no chasing / emotional stake increases (15) · review completed (10). DQS is the number to maximize over a season.

Honest limits

This app prices from your manual inputs and the odds you copy from Hard Rock — it has no injury feeds, lineups, sharp-money data, or live stats. Player props and exotic SGP legs need a probability from you. Treat outputs as a disciplined second opinion, not ground truth.
